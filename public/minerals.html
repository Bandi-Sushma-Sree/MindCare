Essential Nutrients for Mental Health

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Use: Essential for brain health, reducing inflammation, and supporting heart health.
Sources: Fatty fish (like salmon and mackerel), flaxseeds, chia seeds, and walnuts

Iron

Use: Essential for the production of hemoglobin, which carries oxygen in the blood.
Sources: Red meat, beans, lentils, spinach, and fortified cereals.

Zinc

Use: Supports immune function, wound healing, and DNA synthesis.
Sources: Meat, shellfish, legumes, and seeds.

Calcium

Use: Necessary for strong bones and teeth, muscle function, and nerve signaling.
Sources: Dairy products, leafy greens, almonds, and fortified foods.

Potassium

Use: Regulates fluid balance, muscle contractions, and nerve signals.
Sources: Bananas, oranges, potatoes, and tomatoes.

Magnesium

Use: Supports muscle and nerve function, energy production, and bone health.
Sources: Nuts, seeds, whole grains, and leafy greens.

Casein

Use: Slow-digesting protein that provides a steady release of amino acids.
Sources: Milk and dairy products.

Whey Protein

Use: Fast-digesting protein that helps with muscle repair and growth.
Sources: Milk and whey protein supplements.

Soy Protein

Use: Plant-based protein that contains all essential amino acids.
Sources: Soybeans, tofu, tempeh, and soy milk.

Collagen

Use: Supports skin elasticity, joint health, and muscle strength.
Sources: Bone broth, collagen supplements, and certain fish.

Vitamin E

Use: Antioxidant that protects cells from damage and supports immune function.
Sources: Nuts, seeds, spinach, and vegetable oils.

Vitamin D

Use: Essential for bone health and calcium absorption.
Sources: Sunlight, fortified milk, fatty fish, and egg yolks.

Vitamin C

Use: Antioxidant that boosts the immune system, helps with collagen production, and improves iron absorption.
Sources: Citrus fruits, strawberries, bell peppers, and broccoli.

Vitamin B12

Use: Supports nerve function, red blood cell production, and DNA synthesis.
Sources: Meat, fish, dairy products, and fortified cereals.

Vitamin A

Use: Important for vision, immune function, and skin health.
Sources: Carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach, and liver.

Fiber

Use: Aids digestion, helps maintain healthy blood sugar levels, and supports heart health.
Sources: Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, and legumes.
