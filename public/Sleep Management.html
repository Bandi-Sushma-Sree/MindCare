Recognizing Insomnia: Common Symptoms

Difficulty Falling Asleep

Struggling to fall asleep, lying awake for hours, or waking up frequently.

Daytime Fatigue

Feeling tired, groggy, or lacking energy during the day.

Mood Disturbances

Irritability, anxiety, depression, or mood swings due to lack of sleep.

Understanding Insomnia: Common Triggers

Caffeine and Nicotine

Consuming caffeine and nicotine before bedtime can disrupt sleep patterns.

Screen Time Before Bed

Using electronic devices before bedtime can interfere with sleep due to blue light exposure.

Sleep Environment

An uncomfortable sleeping environment, including noise, temperature, and lighting, can disrupt sleep.

Medical Conditions

Chronic pain, sleep apnea, and other medical conditions can contribute to insomnia.

Lifestyle Factors

Irregular sleep schedules, travel, or night shifts can affect sleep quality.

Dietary Factors

Heavy meals, caffeine, and sugary foods before bedtime can harm sleep quality.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I)

Effective, Long-term Solution for Insomnia

CBT-I is a structured program that helps you identify and replace thoughts and behaviors that cause or worsen sleep problems with habits that promote sound sleep. Unlike sleeping pills, CBT-I helps you overcome the underlying causes of your sleep problems.

  • Addresses cognitive factors
  • Improves sleep behaviors
  • Long-lasting results
  • Reduces reliance on sleep medications

Effective Strategies for Better Sleep

Establish a Consistent Sleep Schedule

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, including weekends.

Practice Relaxation Techniques Before Bed

Try techniques like deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, or meditation to calm your mind.

Create a Sleep-Conducive Environment

Make your bedroom dark, quiet, and cool to promote better sleep.

Limit Screen Time Before Bed

Avoid screens for at least an hour before bedtime to reduce blue light exposure.

Maintain a Healthy Lifestyle

Regular exercise, a balanced diet, and stress management can improve sleep quality.
